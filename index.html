I teach energy-balancing, nurturing Vinyasa yoga in the lineage of Dr. Liz Lindh and T.K.S. Desikachar. I combine scientific and intuitive knowledge to help my students connect with the forces within and around them. A focus of my yoga is counteracting the physical, mental, and spiritual stressors encountered by scientists, academics, and other intellectuals.

My personal mission is to help people to be better stewards of the Earth, themselves, and each other. I achieve this by exploring the connections between life and the Earth (through my work as a marine biogeochemist), empowering the next generation of leaders and thinkers in the environmental sciences (as an assistant professor at the University of Southern California), and cultivating a culture of wellness in academia (as a yoga teacher and holder of space).

I am a RYT-200. I also hold a PhD in microbial biogeochemistry and chemical oceanography from MIT and the Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution. I am currently based in Zurich, Switzerland and from January 2024 will be in the Los Angeles area.

You can contact me about private yoga/wellness classes and workshops for yourself, or for a group.
